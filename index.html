Editor's Letter

I remember getting one of my first nuggets of fool’s gold, during a summer roadtrip to the Black Hills. The sun was fiery hot that afternoon, just like my developing obsession with rocks. Between sightseeing, I would peer out the backseat car window and notify my dad of gift shops on the side of the road. Once inside, my parents would try to redirect me to other stones and fossils, but I was enchanted with the cheap shine of fool’s gold. Not only was fool’s gold a bargain, but I was certain it was the best rock in my velvet satchel of stones. What can I say, I was an early connoisseur of value!

Nearly twenty years have passed since that day, and a lot has changed. For one, I’ve accepted that Santa probably bought my dud of a rock tumbler from Fleet Farm, and I have forgiven him for that. But some things have remained the same, I still love a good deal. For a number of years, this itch was scratched by hunting down treasures at thrift stores or garage sales. More recently, however, I have found myself longing for fun, creative outlets that would actually add value to my day to day life - not just accumulating shit. Whether it's learning a new cocktail recipe or a thrifty take on a trending house project, we could all use a little more practical magic in our lives.

Fools' Gold (plural) is our attempt at making inspiring content for fools like you and me. We don't have all the time or money, so we've gotta get creative! In a day and age, where social media can leave you feeling overwhelmed and inadequate, it’s our goal to give you ideas that are not only accessible, but leave you with a sense of excitement… like fool’s gold! You can count on discovering original material for your home, foolproof recipes, and unique perspectives on DIY projects + travel. We also believe part of the magic lies in the experience. Getting my first chunk of fool’s gold wouldn’t have been nearly as memorable, if I was simply gifted it - the sweat, dust and search are all part of the memory. Which is why, we encourage you to slow down and relish in the experience. So, pour yourself a drink (or bowl of cereal) and enjoy!

Your Fool
